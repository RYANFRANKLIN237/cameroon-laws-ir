(2) The contract of employment, may, while still in force, be amended on the initiative of either
party.
(a) Where the amendment suggested by the employer is substantial and is rejected by the
worker, the termination of the contract that may result therefrom shall be the responsibility
of the employer. Such termination shall be wrongful only where it is not justified by the
interest of the undertaking.
(b) Where the amendment suggested by the worker is substantial and is rejected by the
employer, the contract may be terminated only following the resignation of the worker.
