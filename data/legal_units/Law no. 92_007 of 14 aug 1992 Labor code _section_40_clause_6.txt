(6)
(a) Where the parties fail to reach an agreement after the negotiations provided for above,
or where notwithstanding the measures envisaged certain dismissals are still necessary, the
employer must determine an order of dismissals taking into consideration professional
proficiency, seniority in the undertaking and the family responsibilities of workers. In any
case, the order of dismissals must give precedence to professional proficiency.
(b) To obtain the opinions and suggestions of the staff representatives, the employers shall
send them the list of workers he intends the dismiss, explaining the criteria used.
(c) The staff representatives shall be bound to forward their written reply to the employer
within no more than eight clear days.
(d) The employer's notification and the reply from the staff representatives shall
immediately be sent to the Labour Inspector of the area for arbitration.
